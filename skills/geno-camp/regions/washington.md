# Washington State — Campsite Region Profile

## Booking System

**Platform:** GoingToCamp — washington.goingtocamp.com
**Phone:** (888) CAMPOUT / (888) 226-7688
**Advance window:** 9 months
**Same-day:** Available until 2pm
**Pass required:** Discover Pass ($45/year or $10/day) for all state parks

## Regions

### Olympic Peninsula (Forks, Port Angeles, Sequim)
- **Vibe:** Rainforest, rugged coast, old growth, wildlife
- **Best for:** Beach camping, rainforest immersion, remote feel
- **Shower availability:** Excellent on private sites (Hipcamp), limited in Olympic NP
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - Shangri La Push (Hipcamp, 98%, free hot showers, near Second Beach)
  - Olympic Adventure Campground (Hipcamp, 91%, $5/guest showers)
  - Crescent Beach & RV (Hipcamp, 94%, quarter-operated showers)
  - Sol Duc Rainforest Retreat (Hipcamp, 92%, outdoor showers)
- **Note:** Olympic NP campgrounds (Hoh, Kalaloch, Sol Duc) have NO showers. Nearest showers at Bogachiel State Park or private sites.

### San Juan Islands & Puget Sound (Anacortes, Orcas, Whidbey)
- **Vibe:** Island hopping, marine wildlife, scenic bridges, kayaking
- **Best for:** Family camping, boating, whale watching
- **Shower availability:** State parks have good facilities
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - Deception Pass State Park (326 sites, coin-op showers $1/4 tokens, 4.5★)
  - Moran State Park (Orcas Island, 151 sites, 10 showers, Mt. Constitution views)
  - Camano Island State Park (77 sites, restrooms with showers)
  - Fort Casey State Park (Whidbey Island, showers available)
  - Fort Ebey State Park (62 sites with electric, showers)
  - Larrabee State Park (6 showers, first state park in WA)

### North Cascades (Marblemount, Rockport, Winthrop)
- **Vibe:** Alpine, glaciers, remote valleys, crystal lakes
- **Best for:** Backcountry access, photography, solitude
- **Shower availability:** Limited — NPS campgrounds have none; some private sites do
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - Pearrygin Lake State Park (Winthrop, 147 sites, free hot showers, lakefront)
  - Lil Bit O' Heaven Camping (Marblemount, Hipcamp, private shower)
  - Hideaway Cedars Campground (Marblemount, Hipcamp, hot shower)
- **Note:** Newhalem Creek and Colonial Creek (North Cascades NP) have flush toilets but NO showers.

### Mount Rainier Area (Ashford, Elbe, Packwood)
- **Vibe:** Volcanic peak, wildflower meadows, old growth, waterfalls
- **Best for:** Day hiking from camp, stargazing, nature photography
- **Shower availability:** NO showers at any Mt. Rainier NP campground
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - La Wis Wis Campground (USFS, near Packwood — verify showers)
  - Riverside Camping Near Mt Rainier (Hipcamp, Elbe, 96%, 15 min to park)
  - Mounthaven Resort (private, Ashford — cabins with private showers)
- **Alternative:** Day-use showers at Ashford businesses or Packwood rec facilities

### Central Cascades / I-90 Corridor (Snoqualmie, Cle Elum, Easton)
- **Vibe:** Easy access from Seattle, mountain lakes, river camping
- **Best for:** Weekend trips from Seattle, family camping
- **Shower availability:** State parks have showers; USFS sites usually don't
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - Lake Easton State Park (148 sites, 4 restrooms with showers, I-90 access)
  - Lake Wenatchee State Park (hot showers, lake access, 197 sites)
- **Note:** Lake Easton standard campground may be partially closed for cleanup in 2026 — check status before booking.

### Eastern Washington (Spokane, Coulee, Ellensburg)
- **Vibe:** Dry, sunny, desert landscapes, coulees, big lakes
- **Best for:** Guaranteed sun, water sports, geology
- **Shower availability:** Good at state parks
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - Sun Lakes-Dry Falls State Park (free hot showers, 12 showers, geological wonder)
  - Riverside State Park (Spokane, showers, river camping)
  - Pearrygin Lake State Park (see North Cascades above — technically eastern WA climate)

### Southwest WA / Coast (Long Beach, Westport, Ocean Shores)
- **Vibe:** Windswept beaches, lighthouses, clamming, kite flying
- **Best for:** Beach camping, storm watching, family beach days
- **Shower availability:** Good at coastal state parks
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - Cape Disappointment State Park (14 showers, 4 ADA — CLOSED until June 30 2026 for renovations)
  - Twin Harbors Beach State Park (showers, 172 acres on Pacific coast)
  - Grayland Beach State Park (120 sites, showers, oceanfront)
  - Ocean City State Park (9 showers, beach access)

## Seasonal Notes

- **Peak season:** July 4 through Labor Day — book 9 months ahead for popular parks
- **Shoulder season:** June 1-30, Sep 15-Oct 15 — good availability, cooler but pleasant
- **Fire restrictions:** Common in Eastern WA and Cascades from late July. Check wa.gov fire info.
- **Mosquitoes:** Worst June-July in wet areas (Olympic rainforest, mountain lakes). Eastern WA is drier.
- **Discovery Pass:** Required at ALL state parks. Buy online or at REI/sporting goods stores. $45/year is worth it if camping more than 4 days total.
