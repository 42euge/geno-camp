# Oregon — Campsite Region Profile

## Booking System

**Platform:** Oregon State Parks / ReserveAmerica — oregonstateparks.reserveamerica.com
**Phone:** (800) 452-5687
**Advance window:** 9 months
**Same-day:** Available at some parks
**Pass required:** No day-use pass required at most state parks (unlike WA). Some trailheads require Northwest Forest Pass ($5/day or $30/year).

## Regions

### Oregon Coast (Astoria, Cannon Beach, Newport, Bandon, Brookings)
- **Vibe:** Dramatic sea stacks, tide pools, rainforest-meets-ocean, charming coastal towns
- **Best for:** Beach camping, storm watching, whale watching, tide pooling
- **Shower availability:** Excellent — Oregon coastal state parks have well-maintained shower buildings
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - Fort Stevens State Park (Astoria, 476 sites, free hot showers, bike trails, beach, wreck of the Peter Iredale)
  - Cape Lookout State Park (Tillamook, 173 sites, hot showers, 2.5mi cape trail, whale watching)
  - Beverly Beach State Park (Newport, 253 sites, hot showers, beach access, near Oregon Coast Aquarium)
  - Sunset Bay State Park (Coos Bay, 108 sites, hot showers, sheltered bay, Shore Acres botanical garden nearby)
  - Harris Beach State Park (Brookings, 149 sites, hot showers, sea stacks, best weather on the coast)
- **Note:** Oregon coast is cooler and wetter than you'd expect in summer. Bring layers even in July/August.

### Columbia River Gorge (Hood River, Cascade Locks, The Dalles)
- **Vibe:** Waterfalls, wind sports, craft beer, scenic hwy
- **Best for:** Waterfall hikes, windsurfing/kiteboarding, brewery tours
- **Shower availability:** Mixed — state parks have showers; USFS campgrounds usually don't
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - Ainsworth State Park (35 sites, hot showers, close to Multnomah Falls, I-84 access)
  - Memaloose State Park (The Dalles, 99 sites, hot showers, river views, quiet)
  - Viento State Park (Hood River, 56 sites, hot showers, windsurfing access)
- **Note:** The Gorge gets strong afternoon winds — great for wind sports, challenging for tent camping. Stake everything down.

### Mount Hood & Cascades (Government Camp, Bend, Sisters)
- **Vibe:** Volcanic peaks, alpine lakes, mountain biking, craft beer (Bend)
- **Best for:** Hiking, mountain biking, skiing (year-round at Timberline), lake swimming
- **Shower availability:** Limited at USFS sites; good at state parks and private campgrounds
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - Tumalo State Park (Bend, 68 sites, hot showers, Deschutes River, 5 min from Bend)
  - LaPine State Park (La Pine, 128 sites, hot showers, tallest ponderosa pine in OR, Newberry Volcanic Monument)
  - Trillium Lake (USFS, Government Camp — NO showers but iconic Mt. Hood reflection)
- **Alternative:** Bend has numerous shower-equipped private campgrounds and day-use facilities.

### Central Oregon (Bend, Redmond, Prineville, Madras)
- **Vibe:** High desert, juniper, rimrock, dark skies, sunny
- **Best for:** Rock climbing (Smith Rock), stargazing, mountain biking, reservoir swimming
- **Shower availability:** State parks have showers; USFS/BLM sites don't
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - The Cove Palisades State Park (Madras, 91 sites, hot showers, Lake Billy Chinook, houseboating)
  - Skull Hollow (Smith Rock area, BLM — primitive, no showers but close to Smith Rock)
  - Prineville Reservoir State Park (73 sites, hot showers, swimming, fishing)
- **Note:** Central Oregon is high desert — 300+ days of sunshine, hot days and cool nights in summer.

### Southern Oregon (Crater Lake, Ashland, Grants Pass)
- **Vibe:** Volcanic landscapes, whitewater, Shakespeare (Ashland), wild rivers
- **Best for:** Crater Lake, Rogue River rafting, Oregon Shakespeare Festival, wine
- **Shower availability:** Limited near Crater Lake (NPS); state parks elsewhere have showers
- **Top picks with showers:**
  - Valley of the Rogue State Park (Grants Pass, 147 sites, hot showers, Rogue River, I-5 access)
  - Joseph Stewart State Park (Lost Creek Lake, 151 sites, hot showers, lake swimming, near Crater Lake)
  - Mazama Campground (Crater Lake NP, 214 sites — NO showers, flush toilets only)
- **Note:** Crater Lake typically opens late June due to snow. Check road conditions before planning.

### Eastern Oregon (John Day, Wallowa, Steens)
- **Vibe:** Remote, vast, rugged, uncrowded, dark skies
- **Best for:** Solitude, painted hills geology, Wallowa Mountains ("Alps of Oregon"), hot springs
- **Shower availability:** Very limited — most campgrounds are USFS/BLM primitive sites
- **Top picks:**
  - Wallowa Lake State Park (Joseph, 121 sites, hot showers, Wallowa Mountains backdrop, tramway)
  - Page Springs (Steens Mountain, BLM — primitive, no showers, but near Alvord Hot Springs)
  - Clyde Holliday State Park (John Day, 31 sites, hot showers, river, Painted Hills 40 min away)

## Seasonal Notes

- **Peak season:** July 4 through Labor Day — coastal parks and Bend area book quickly
- **Shoulder season:** June, September–October — excellent conditions, good availability
- **Coastal weather:** Even in summer, expect fog and temps in the 60s. Always bring a jacket.
- **Fire season:** Common in Central/Eastern OR from late July. Check oregonsmoke.org.
- **Wildflowers:** Best in June–July at alpine elevations
- **Whale watching:** Gray whales migrate Dec–June; best viewing from Cape Perpetua, Depoe Bay, Cape Meares
- **No day-use pass:** Unlike WA's Discover Pass, most OR state parks don't charge a day-use fee. Some trailheads require a Northwest Forest Pass.
